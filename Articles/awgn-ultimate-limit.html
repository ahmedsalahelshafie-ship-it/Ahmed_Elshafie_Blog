In wireless systems, signal transmission is affected by noise, interference, and fading. While fading introduces randomness that varies the channel quality, AWGN represents a clean and idealized environment where only white Gaussian noise affects the signal. Here, we explain why AWGN sets the performance limit for wireless systems under different antenna configurations, and show the mathematical expressions for each case.

The same conclusion applies to bit error rate (BER) performance, where an AWGN channel always provides better (lower) error rates compared to a fading channel at the same average SNR.

Conclusion

Across SISO, MISO, SIMO, and MIMO systems, the fading channel's average capacity and BER are always worse than those of corresponding AWGN channels. This makes AWGN the ultimate theoretical limit for performance in wireless communication systems. No matter how the channel is exploited, the best performance achievable under fading cannot exceed the performance of an equivalent AWGN system with the same power and bandwidth.
