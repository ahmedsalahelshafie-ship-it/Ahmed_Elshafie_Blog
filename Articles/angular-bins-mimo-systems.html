In wireless MIMO systems such as 5G, beamforming (BF) plays a central role in improving link quality, spatial selectivity, and user multiplexing. A practical implementation of BF relies on the concept of angular bins, which discretize the angular space into a finite set of beam directions. These bins form the foundation of codebook-based feedback and beam management.

When Angular Binning Works Best

Angular binning works very well in sparse channels, such as line-of-sight or mmWave environments, where most of the energy is focused in a small number of directions. Takeaway

Angular bin strategies are critical for practical and scalable MIMO system design. They reduce overhead and complexity, particularly in scenarios where channel paths are few and distinct.
