In digital communication systems, especially those using OFDM like 4G LTE and 5G NR, synchronization is critical. Two major impairments that affect synchronization are clock error and phase error. Both distort the received signal, degrade performance, and increase the EVM.

Synchronization Matters

In a wireless system, the receiver needs to correctly sample the incoming signal and decode its phase. This requires accurate timing and phase alignment between the transmitter and receiver. Any deviation leads to inter-symbol interference, carrier leakage, or subcarrier (SC) distortion, especially in OFDM where precise alignment is vital.

Clock Errors

Clock error refers to the mismatch in sampling rates between the transmitter and the receiver. This causes a timing drift, which means the receiver samples the signal at incorrect instants. There are two main types:

  • Sampling Clock Offset (SCO): A slight difference in clock frequency leads to a slow shift in sampling time.
  • Sampling Clock Phase Error: The receiver starts sampling at the wrong time instant, causing constant phase distortion.

In OFDM, clock errors destroy orthogonality between subcarriers, causing inter-carrier interference (ICI).

Phase Errors

Phase error happens when there's a mismatch between the transmitter and receiver oscillator phases. It can be:

  • Constant phase offset: The receiver sees a constant rotation of the signal constellation.
  • Phase noise: Rapid, random fluctuations in the carrier phase, due to oscillator instability.
  • Carrier Frequency Offset (CFO): A frequency mismatch causes a linear phase drift over time.

Phase errors rotate the signal constellation and spread subcarrier energy across others, increasing EVM and bit errors.

5G Systems

3GPP specifications such as TS 38.101 set strict limits on EVM for different modulations. These limits indirectly account for allowed clock and phase errors.

Receivers use techniques like pilot tracking, CFO correction, and PLL-based synchronization to correct these impairments dynamically.

Summary

Clock and phase errors are powerful distortions in wireless systems. They blur symbol timing and rotate signal phases, reducing data reliability. Understanding and compensating them is essential for achieving the high throughput and reliability targets of modern wireless systems like 5G.
