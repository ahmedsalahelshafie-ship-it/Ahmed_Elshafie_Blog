About The Author

Ahmed Elshafie is a Wireless Systems Engineer at Apple and an IEEE Senior Member. Over the years, he has authored more than 288 granted U.S. patent and filed over 690 U.S. patent applications, and more than 1,196 patent applications globally.

His academic journey began with a B.Sc. (with honors) in Electrical Engineering from Alexandria University, Egypt, in 2009, followed by a Master's degree in Communication and Information Technology from Nile University, Cairo, in 2014. He earned a Ph.D. from The University of Texas at Dallas in 2018.

Before joining Apple, Ahmed worked as a Senior/Staff Systems Engineer at Qualcomm Technologies in San Diego from February 2018 to March 2023. During his tenure, he contributed to key product developments and represented Qualcomm as an R&D delegate in RAN1. His work was recognized through multiple Qualcomm Star (Qualstar) Awards, underscoring his contributions to the company's advancements.

Throughout his career, Ahmed has been honored with several academic and professional accolades, including the David Daniel Best Doctoral Thesis Award (2018), the Jonsson School Industrial Advisory Council Fellowship Award (2017), and the IEEE Transactions on Communications Exemplary Reviewer Award for three consecutive years (2015‚Äì2017), among others. He was also nominated for the 2018 CGS/ProQuest Distinguished Dissertation Award.

He actively contributes to the research community through multiple editorial positions, currently serving as an Editor for the IEEE Transactions on Communications, IEEE Communications Surveys & Tutorials, and the IEEE Internet of Things Journal, as well as a Senior Editor for IEEE Communications Letters. His past editorial experience includes service with Physical Communications, the IEEE Open Journal of the Communications Society, and Transactions on Emerging Telecommunications Technologies, along with a guest editorship for the IEEE Transactions on Cognitive Communications and Networking.
